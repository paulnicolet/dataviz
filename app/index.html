Visualising world temperatures

It is not new that the climate system is warming as multiple scientific evidences have proved. But can we leverage the new growing branch of science known as data science to demonstrate the same phenomena ?

Climate change does not respect border; it does not respect who you are - rich and poor, small and big. Therefore, this is what we call “global challenges“, which require global solidarity. – Ban Ki-moon

Goal

We aim at providing a simple and user-friendly tool in order to visually explore climate change data. Ideally, the goal is to give insights about how temperatures are evolving on Earth to anyone curious about global warming, using data visualisation techniques. One should note that this work is a tentative to extract meaningful insights, but results are obviously not guaranteed.

Data

  • Temperatures dataset
  • Population dataset
  • Growth domestic product dataset

Libraries

  • Visualisation: d3.js, c3.js
  • Preprocessing: pandas
  • Web: materialize

Temperatures time series

This chart presents the temperature evolution for multiple country or continent from 1950 to 2013 with regression to help guessing the tendency. Since temperatures depend on geography, it is possible to normalize the chart in order to compare several entities.

Temperatures map

With the world map, it is possible to look at world temperatures in function of geographical coordinates. It is possible to explore data from 1750 even if a few points are available. One can hover the points to get more details.

Evolution speed

The bubble chart aims at looking at speed of temperature evolution between countries, correlated with two other variables: population and Growth Domestic Product. Motion of bubbles give insights about the amount and speed of change of each variable.
